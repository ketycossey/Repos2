Buiti achüluruni

Garifuna, also known as Garinagu, are the descendants of an Afro-indigenous population from the Caribbean island of St Vincent who were exiled to the Honduran coast in the eighteenth century and subsequently moved to Belize and Guatemala. Garifuna mainly live on the coast but are also very present in towns and villages.

The language

The garifuna languange has an interesting story. Most people would describe it as a dialect of French, Yoruba, Spanish and Arawak. Let's learn some Garifuna phrases together.

Phrase Garifuna
How are you? Ida biña?
What's your name Ka biri?
My name is niri bai
Thank you Seremein
Leave me alone Igara bana nungua!
